Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students
The Do's:
* Do think before you post: Always consider how your posts, comments, or photos might be viewed by future employers, internship coordinators, or professors.
* Do review your privacy settings: Regularly check and update your privacy settings to control who has access to your personal life and updates.
* Do engage constructively: Use social media to build a positive digital footprint by networking with peers, joining academic groups, and sharing insightful industry news.
* Do verify information before sharing: Check the credibility of news or articles before forwarding or reposting them to avoid spreading misinformation.
* Do keep professional and personal boundaries: Maintain distinct boundaries between professional profiles (like LinkedIn) and personal accounts (like Instagram or Snapchat).
The Don'ts:
* Don't post offensive or inappropriate content: Avoid sharing anything discriminatory, illegal, or highly controversial that could permanently damage your reputation.
* Don't complain publicly about college or peers: Venting about your professors, college administration, or classmates online is unprofessional and can lead to disciplinary action.
* Don't overshare sensitive personal information: Never publicly post your home address, phone number, passwords, or financial details.
* Don't engage in cyberbullying or trolling: Avoid participating in heated public arguments, harassment, or leaving malicious comments on others' posts.
* Don't post others' work without credit: Avoid plagiarism; always give proper credit if you are sharing someone else's art, photography, or written ideas.
